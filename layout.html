ABOUT EFFIEL TOWER

The Eiffel Tower, known as "La Tour Eiffel" in French, is one of the most iconic landmarks in the world, located in Paris, France. Hereâ€™s a detailed description based on various sources:

OVERVIEW

  • Height: The Eiffel Tower stands at a remarkable height of 1,083 feet (330 meters), making it the tallest structure in Paris and a prominent feature of the city's skyline. Originally, it was 1,024 feet (312 meters) tall without antennas. The tower's base forms a square measuring 410 feet (125 meters) on each side
  • Construction: Designed by engineer Gustave Eiffel, the tower was constructed between 1887 and 1889 for the Exposition Universelle, a world's fair celebrating the centennial of the French Revolution. It took just over two years to complete, which was considered an engineering marvel at the time
  • Materials: The structure is made of wrought iron and consists of approximately 18,038 individual iron parts connected by over 2.5 million rivets. The total weight of the tower is about 10,000 tons

DESIGN AND STRUCTURE

  • The Eiffel Tower features three levels accessible to visitors:
    • The first level is located at 187 feet (57 meters) and includes restaurants and shops.
    • The second level is at 377 feet (115 meters) and offers panoramic views of Paris.
    • The third level, or observation deck, reaches up to 906 feet (276 meters) above ground, providing breathtaking views of the city
  • The tower also has elevators that transport visitors to different levels, with a total of five lifts operating from the ground to the second level and additional lifts to the top
